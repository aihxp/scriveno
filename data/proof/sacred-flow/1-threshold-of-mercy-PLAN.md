## Purpose

Draft a short commentary passage on the relationship between mercy and accountability.

## Register

Measured, reverent, and careful. Avoid polemic. Avoid final claims where the project has not supplied sources.

## Reader-State Movement

- Start: "Mercy and accountability feel like competing values."
- End: "The passage can be read as holding both together without making either one decorative."

## Source Anchors

- Primary passage: supplied by project source notes.
- Tradition profile: supplied by project framework.
- Doctrinal terms: use only terms already accepted in `DOCTRINES.md`.

## Commentary Beats

1. Name the tension without over-explaining it.
2. Show why mercy is not simple pardon without moral memory.
3. Show why accountability is not simple condemnation.
4. Give the reader a durable interpretive question.
5. Leave source-specific claims for later annotation if not loaded.

## Watchpoints

- Do not invent a named commentator.
- Do not imply a universal tradition consensus.
- Do not use modern psychological language as if it were the passage's own category.
- Do not collapse sacred register into casual advice.
